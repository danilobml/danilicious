Moqueca

4-6 Servings

1h

More effort

Moderately expensive

Quantity Ingredient
⅓ cupFresh lime juice.
½ tsp.Salt.
½ tbsp.Freshly ground black pepper.
2Garlic cloves, minced.
2kgSea bass or halibut fillet, cut into 1/2-inch wide strips.
1kgLarge shrimp, peeled and deveined.
2Eggs, lightly beaten.
2 tbsp.Olive oil.
2 cupsFinely chopped onion.
1 cupFinely chopped green bell pepper.
1 cupFinely chopped red bell pepper.
¾ cupMinced green onions (about 1 bunch).
5Garlic cloves, minced.
1Bay leaf.
2 cupsChopped tomato (about 2 large).
½ cupMinced fresh cilantro.
1 canChicken broth.
1 cupCoconut milk.
¼ tbsp.Ground red pepper.

This traditional Brazilian seafood dish recipe comes from the state of Bahia in northern Brazil. The dish, moqueca de peixe, is a tropical fish stew fragrant with garlic and peppers, and enriched with coconut milk.

Preparation

  • Step 1

    Combine first 6 ingredients in a large bowl; toss to coat. Marinate in refrigerator 30 minutes.

  • Step 2

    Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion, bell peppers, green onions, garlic, and bay leaf; cook 6 minutes, stirring occasionally. Increase heat to medium-high; add tomato, and cook 2 minutes. Add 1/4 cup cilantro, clam juice, and broth. Bring to a boil; reduce heat, and simmer 10 minutes. Discard bay leaf.

  • Step 3

    Place one-third of vegetable mixture in a blender, and puree until smooth. Pour pureed vegetable mixture into pan. Repeat procedure with remaining vegetable mixture. Add coconut milk and red pepper to pureed vegetable mixture. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat; cook 3 minutes. Add fish mixture; cook 3 minutes or until fish is done. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup cilantro.

  • Step 4

    Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 170C or until a piece of bread browns in 30 secs. Fry in batches for 3-4 mins until golden, turning once or twice. Remove and drain on kitchen paper. Serve warm with the parsley scattered over.

Enjoy!
